About

I am just an ordinary guy who watched too much Star Trek as a kid. Growing up, I would always dream about the future of technology and human civilization. Through luck and a bit of hard work, I have been granted the rare privilege of a world class education, and now I am ready to leverage the opportunities available to me and realize my childhood fantasies.

I study physics and computer science at Caltech, and I am interested in quantum computing research, as I believe it will revolutionize our civilization one day. I am currently completing a senior thesis with Professor Goddard in the Materials Simulation Center, where I perform quantum mechanical calculations and study semiconductors. I am very excited about the new AWS quantum computing center at Caltech, and hope to get involved in this intersection of physics and computer science.

I have been an avid programmer for most of my life. I taught myself fullstack development in highschool and completed the machine learning track at Caltech. I have several years of experience applying machine learning techniques and computer vision in both industry and scientific research. I am especially interested in natural language processing, tensor algorithms, and reinforcement learning. I am currently working with Anima Anandkumar, director of ML research at NVIDIA, to try to apply semi-supervised learning techniques to complicated problems in the space of robotic surgery. I have also used my computational and analytical skills to help with ongoing research at Dana-Farber, LIGO, CAST, and NASA/JPL.

However, the greatest passion of my life has always been space exploration, and I hope to some day contribute to advancing humanity toward a future of sailing across the stars. Space is truly the final frontier. Specifically, asteroid mining will be the next crucial stepping stone in humanity’s interstellar destiny, and therefore I have decided that the ultimate goal of my career will be to accelerate the emergence of this industry.

Current Focus: Machine Learning // Data Science // Full Stack
